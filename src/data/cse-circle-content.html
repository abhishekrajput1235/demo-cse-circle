Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Wanted to Ask

Quick answers to the most common questions about optional selection, our programmes, and UPSC preparation.

Is Maths Optional good for non-engineering students?

Yes. Many UPSC toppers with Maths Optional have commerce, humanities, or science backgrounds. What matters is logical aptitude and consistent practice — not an engineering degree. We have success stories from all streams.

How long does it take to prepare Sociology Optional for UPSC?

A focused aspirant can complete the Sociology Optional syllabus in 4–5 months. The advantage is that Paper II (Indian Society) significantly overlaps with GS Paper I and II, reducing effective preparation time further.

Are PYQs on The CSE Circle really free?

Yes, our PYQ section with topic-wise PDFs is available free of charge. We believe access to past papers is a right, not a privilege. Advanced model answers and analysis are part of our paid curriculum.

Which optional scores higher — Maths or Sociology?

Both can score 280–320+ with the right preparation. Maths is more objective and mechanical; Sociology rewards analytical thinking and contemporary application. The best optional is the one that matches your strength and background.

Does The CSE Circle offer online classes?

Yes, all our courses are available online in live + recorded formats. Students from across India — and abroad — access our complete programme digitally with full faculty interaction and doubt-clearing support.

Can I join mid-preparation if I've already started elsewhere?

Absolutely. Many of our students join mid-preparation from other institutes or self-study backgrounds. Our faculty conducts a brief diagnostic session to calibrate where you are and build a customised plan forward.

Is Sociology Optional scoring even with an arts background?

Students with humanities, political science, or social science backgrounds find Sociology Optional extremely natural. But even science and engineering graduates have scored 290+ by following our structured programme diligently.

How is answer writing evaluated at The CSE Circle?

Answers are evaluated by experienced faculty on a 5-dimensional rubric: content accuracy, structure, analytical depth, contemporary examples, and presentation. Detailed written feedback is provided for every answer submitted.
